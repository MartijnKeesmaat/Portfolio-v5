Codename is a series of projects in which UI interactions are explored through visual design and code.

It now consists of 16+ conceptual projects. The first project was uploaded at the end of 2018. Each project has a different concept but involves a unique mechanic, interaction, or effect. There will be a preview of a selection of projects, since the page would otherwise be huge.

These are my most personal projects and align the most with what type of work I want to create. It's the combining of various ways of creating which makes it such a fun type of project to work on. In the future, I want to involve 3D, WebGL, canvas for the tech-side, and more unique and meaningful experiences on the conceptual side concepts.

Feb 10, 2020

Sans language

This one is a more personal project. The project is inspired by the idea of losing a passion. It's a horrible empty feeling. Working on it was like therapy. Through the process, I refound parts of my love for creating. It was created after a while of not feeling able to do anything. The title is inspired by this: "without language, there is no thought". Without is replaced with the french word "sans" from sans-serif, since it's a type-focussed project.

I reflected on the projects which used to give me energy by writing. Writing has been a great way to explore those thoughts. Which is also why type is important in the project. The answer was a project like this. Combining visual design with creative coding, talking about something that is personal to me. Using large typography, interesting layouts, and unique interactions.
