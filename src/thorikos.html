Thousands of ancient greek findings visualised in a dynamic representation by combining the current system with modern age technologies.

ThorikΩs is a project created for the Information Design course of the Amsterdam University of Applied Sciences The ThorikΩs visualization was created in collaboration with the UU. Thorikos was a city in the southern portion of ancient Attica. There have been found all sorts of objects from ancient Greece in this area. These findings consist mostly of shards of objects like tiles, amphora's, jugs and cups.

The findings have been categorized and are visualized manually. Creating those visualizations takes a lot of work which is why this project is created. With the ThorikΩs project, we can dynamically create map views of specific findings. Like Amphora found in 2013, poorly reserved grinding stones with a black glaze or drinking vessels from the Archaic period.

The project was received with pleasure by teachers and more importantly the client. We received the highest possible grade, a 10, and the project was chosen as most useful by the client. The tool is now being used by the ThorikΩs team to gain new insights.

A feature

Browse Through The Collection

You start with the entire collection. This is represented by the green rectangle. You can divide the collection into categories seen below. For example, when you click shape objects, you will see the main objects like tiles, amphora's, jugs and cups.

These can then be discovered further. Once you click one of these objects you will reduce the objects to your selection. This process can be repeated over and over until there are no more findings.

A feature

Focus On The Smaller Details

Some categories contain a lot of variables. The shape objects, for example, contain 144 different types of objects. By zooming in, you remove the 10 biggest and fill the screen with the remaining variables.

One plus one

Equals Thorikos

This tool is an interactive version of two tools combined. The first is the original map with grid system of Thorikos. This was a analog system and needed to be digitalised. This new digital system is then combined with a interactive treemap. The treemap was “hacked” to become a filter system.

The Thorikos site has tons of variables of which we choose the 7 most important. Some of these had 100+ sub-categories. The interactive treemap can store all these variables and serve an unique map view for each selection.

A feature

Explore The Map

Once you are happy with your selection, you can flip over to the map view. The map view show the map of Thorikos along with a grid of the areas that have been searched. The grid is divided into meso squares, these are areas of 25x25 meter.

The meso squares are group by macro squares (50x50) and can be shown by toggling the grid. If you hover over the meso squares you see the number of findings.

We crafted a puzzle which matched the digital product. You can follow along with this puzzle by taking filtering the data on the site. At the last step you see 4 findings at the same location as the site in Thorikos. The puzzle is created by lasercutting and engraving.

A feature

Keep Track of your Progress

We've added breadcrumbs to keep track of where you currently are in the collection. These can be found in the upper-left area above the visualizations. Every time you browse the collection it's stored and shown here. The breadcrumbs are interactive in both the collection view and map view. Once you click them you navigate to that selection.

A feature

Share your Findings

If you want to share your findings you can click the button in the upper-right area and download the map view. You will then receive a .png image that contains the grid, map, legend, and breadcrumbs.
